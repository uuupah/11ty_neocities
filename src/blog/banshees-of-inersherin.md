so much of this film feels like masculine posturing in a world where it's becoming more and more obvious that it's going out of style. when both colm cuts off his fingers, trudges to the house, and then walks back with his bloody stump (in front of padraic), an dpadraic goes through with burning down colms house, they're both showing that they do what they mean and they don't let slights go unpunished, even when they know that nothing good will come of it and it will actively hurt them. padruic checking colms house after setting it ablaze shows how he actually does care, but he's letting the opportunity to do what he wants to do in his heart slip away because it's easier to just have "a code" and blindly stick to it instead of admitting any vulnerability 

I also like the legacy vs being appreciated in your lifetime comparison. while i think that striving for legacy specifically is stupid, I totally understand colms reaching for it given his age. I do _also_ appreciate Siobhan correcting him on Mozart being from the 18th century - he definitely sees himself as more worldly than Padraic, and definitely is, but he's not infallible. he's so set on this that he would destroy his own chances of attaining it by maiming himself to _prove_ how serious it is. maybe he thought he was a bit of a van gogh type who had to physically ruin his body for his art. I did also appreciate the priest at the end asking him about his "despair". it's almost a bit difficult to see whether he's going through it or whether this was just what people were like sometimes on a quiet island a hundred years ago

- i love Barry keoghan, he does an amazing job of physical acting for a guy who's uncertain and unconfident and probably a bit stupid. you forget that he's just a normal dude
- Siobhan feels so human. she's so taken when Padraic calls her nice, he tells off colm for being a bullheaded idiot, and she decides enough is enough and that she doesn't want to live on an island surrounded by bitter old men. 
- the view is fucking incredible. imagine that pub being your regular, looking over the gorgeous coastline.
- I love the line "if punching a police office is a son we might as well all just pack it in now"
- it was beautiful to hear a man you don't meet every day by that vocalist - I've only heard real life friends sing it
